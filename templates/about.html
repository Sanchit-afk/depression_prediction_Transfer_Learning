BOOKS

The Noonday Demon: An Atlas of Depression
Andrew Solomon

Solomon calls his book “An Atlas of Depression”. This is everything you would ever want to know about depression—the personal experience of it, the medical experience of it, the pharmacological treatments, the history of it, the cultural interpretations of it, and of course, Solomon’s own struggles with it.

Feeling Good: The New Mood Therapy
David Burns

“This is what three months with a CBT therapist would be like,” book, full of enough exercises to fill a small notebook, you’d have Feeling Good. Burns has done a fantastic job of essentially writing the closest replacement to a real therapist.If anyone needs a therapist, this book is the best recommendation.

Self-Compassion: The Proven Power of Being Kind to Yourself
Kristin Neff

Self-compassion gives you the answer and the how-to for every time you’ve ever heard someone say, “hey, don’t be so hard on yourself.” In this book, Neff proposes self-compassion as a more effective measurement of psychological health and did the research into how we get there.
